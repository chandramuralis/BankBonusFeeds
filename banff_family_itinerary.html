Banff Gondola — Sulphur Mountain
2.5 hrs
📍
Address: 100 Mountain Ave, Banff, AB T1L 1J3
🚗
Parking: Do NOT drive to the Gondola ($17.50 fee). Park FREE at Banff Train Station lot (Spirit Ave & Lynx St). Take Roam Transit Route 1 — FREE with Gondola ticket. Bus every 15–20 min from Banff Ave stops. ~10 min ride.
🎫
Tickets: Pre-book online at banffjaspercollection.com. Opens 10:00 AM — book 10 AM slot. Kids 5 & under always free. Family Experience: 1 child free per paying adult for first 2 hrs (10–12 PM).
👀
What to see: 8-min gondola ride up 700 m. At the top: 360° rooftop deck, Above Banff Interpretive Centre, multi-sensory theatre, Sky Bistro, 500 m boardwalk to Sanson Peak. Dress warm — 5–8°C cooler at summit.
📸
Photo spot: Rooftop observation deck — Banff town, Bow Valley, and 6 mountain ranges in view. Also great inside the gondola cabin on the way up. 📸 Photo perfect

Johnston Canyon — Lower Falls
1.5 hrs
📍
Address: Bow Valley Pkwy, Banff NP, AB (Johnston Canyon trailhead)
🚗

45 min
📍
Address: End of Yoho Valley Rd, Yoho NP, Field, BC V0A 1G0
🚗

Peyto Lake — Bow Summit Viewpoint
45 min
📍
Address: Bow Summit, Icefields Pkwy, Banff NP, AB (highest point on the Parkway, 2,069 m)
🚗
Parking: Free large lot at Bow Summit. No fee. Fills completely by 9:30–10 AM in June. At 9:10 AM you will get a spot. Arriving late = 30–45 min queue wait.
👀
What to see: Peyto Lake is a wolf/fox-head shaped glacier lake far below — unnaturally vivid turquoise colour from glacial flour in the water. Main viewpoint is a 10-min gentle walk from parking on paved/gravel path. Fully accessible.
📸
Photo spot: Main railed viewpoint platform — best early morning (9 AM) for cloud-free light. The lake shape is only visible from above — this is the shot. 📸 Best view on the entire Parkway

Columbia Icefield — Ice Explorer
2 hrs
📍
Address: AB-93, Improvement District No. 12, AB (Columbia Icefield Discovery Centre)
🚗
Parking: Free large lot at the Discovery Centre. No fee. No issues at 10:30 AM.
🎫
Tickets: Book the Glacier Adventure (Ice Explorer bus) in advance at banffjaspercollection.com. Opens 10:30 AM.
👀
What to see: Large modified buses drive up the glacier and onto the Athabasca Glacier surface. Step on 10,000-year-old ice. Drink glacier meltwater from a small stream on the ice. Surreal experience for kids.
🧥
Dress: Warm jacket, gloves, closed-toe shoes. Near 0°C on the glacier with wind chill, even in June.
📸
Photo spot: On the glacier surface with the Ice Explorer bus behind + mountain wall ahead. Blue stake markers show the best photography zone. 📸 Photo perfect

1 hr
